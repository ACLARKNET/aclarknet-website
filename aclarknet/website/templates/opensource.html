Alex Clark's Contributions

In 2004, Alex Clark was inspired by the Plone CMS project to make his first contributions to open source. For the next few years, he contributed to several Plone add-ons including Products.PloneHelpCenter, Products.PloneSoftwareCenter, and Products.PloneOrg.

In 2009, he completed his first Plone Improvement Proposal: Add ability to choose group when adding new site members.

In 2010, he created Pillow (a fork of the Python Imaging Library by Fredrik Lundh). As of early 2017, Pillow has been downloaded over 15 million times, can be found in many major Linux distributions such as Ubuntu & Fedora Core, and is used by Fortune-100-serving companies like Atlassian.

In 2011, he completed his second Plone Improvement Proposal: Add 'test mail server' form to Mail Settings.

In 2013, in an effort to simplify Plone installation, he began the Plock project.

This website launched in early 2015 & last updated in early 2017 is open source.
