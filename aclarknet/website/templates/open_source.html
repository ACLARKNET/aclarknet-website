    Alex Clark's Contributions

    In 2004, inspired by Plone, Alex Clark made his first contributions to open source.

    For the next few years, he contributed to several Plone add-ons including Products.PloneHelpCenter, Products.PloneSoftwareCenter, and Products.PloneOrg.

    In 2009, he completed his first Plone Improvment Proposal: Add ability to choose group when adding new site members.

    In 2010, he created Pillow (a fork of the Python Imaging Library by Fredrik Lundh). As of early 2016, Pillow has been downloaded over 12 million times, can be found in many major Linux distributions such as Ubuntu & Fedora Core, and is used by Fortune-100-serving companies like Atlassian.

    In 2011, he completed his second Plone Improvement Proposal: Add 'test mail server' form to Mail Settings.

    In 2013, in an effort to simplify Plone he started the Plock project.

    This website launched in early 2015 & updated in early 2016 is open source.
