    Open Source

    Alex Clark's contributions to open source

    In 2004, inspired by Plone Alex Clark began contributing to open source.

    For the next several years, he made significant contributions to several Plone add-ons including Products.PloneHelpCenter, Products.PloneSoftwareCenter, and Products.PloneOrg.

    In 2009, he completed his first Plone Improvment Proposal: Add ability to choose group when adding new site members.

    In 2010, he created Pillow (a fork of the Python Imaging Library by Fredrik Lundh). As of early 2016, Pillow has been downloaded over 12 million times and can be found in many major Linux distributions (instead of PIL) including Ubuntu & Fedora Core.

    In 2011, he completed his second Plone Improvement Proposal: Add 'test mail server' form to Mail Settings.

    In 2013, in an effort to simplify Plone he began the Plock project.

    This website launched in early 2015 & updated in early 2016 is open source.
